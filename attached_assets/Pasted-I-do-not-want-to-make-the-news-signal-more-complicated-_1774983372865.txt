I do not want to make the news signal more complicated by mixing article sentiment with price movement.

I want to keep the UX simple and intuitive.

## Direction

For the news cards, I want the label to mean only one thing:

* **Bullish** = this article reads as positive for the stock
* **Bearish** = this article reads as negative for the stock
* **Neutral** = this article is mixed, unclear, low-impact, or mostly informational

So this label should represent **article interpretation**, not market reaction.

## Important

My issue is not that the system is too simple.
My issue is that the current classification quality feels wrong.

Sometimes I read the headline and the “Why it matters” text and it feels clearly positive, but the site labels it bearish.
That hurts trust.

## What I want

Please keep the single-label system, but make the classification logic more refined and more professional.

Use the actual article meaning more carefully:

* headline
* summary / why it matters
* whether the content implies a clearly positive effect, clearly negative effect, or an ambiguous / mixed effect

If the signal is not clearly positive or negative, prefer **neutral**.

I want the output to feel:

* simpler
* more intuitive
* more trustworthy
* more aligned with how a thoughtful human would read the article

## UX goal

I want users to feel:
“Ah, this article is basically positive / negative / neutral for the stock.”

Not:
“Why is this labeled the opposite of what I just read?”

If useful, you can also add a very short supporting reason under the label, such as:

* product competitiveness improvement
* demand concern
* mixed / wait for confirmation

But keep the UI simple.

## Additional requirements

Please make sure this works properly across **all supported languages**, not just one or two.
The classification logic, labels, supporting reasons, and related UI should stay fully language-aware and consistent in English, Korean, Chinese, and future languages as well.

Please also keep the implementation **clean, modular, and Vercel-friendly**:

* avoid hardcoded language-specific logic scattered across components
* keep the sentiment logic reusable and easy to improve later
* structure it so future migration to VS Code / GitHub / Vercel stays smooth

Before implementing, please show me:

* the revised article sentiment criteria
* how bullish / neutral / bearish will now be determined more carefully
* how you will make it feel more professional without making it more complicated
